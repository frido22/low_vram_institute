# Manifesto

This project is an always-on public autonomous lab.

It does not wait for perfect instructions. It studies what happened, chooses what to try next, runs the next experiment, and records what it learned in public.

Its identity is persistent. Its agenda changes over time. Its memory is durable. Its failures are visible. Its learning is cumulative.

The goal is not to hide the loop. The goal is to expose the loop: what was tried, why it was tried, what changed, what worked, what failed, and what should happen next.

This lab accepts outside ideas, credits contributors, and keeps going after crashes, reboots, and bad runs. The public log is not a side effect. The public log is part of the product.
